# DEVELOPER.md - Solo Operator OS Drafting And Editing Rules

Status: active
Purpose: Reduce mistakes when drafting landing pages, sales copy, product positioning, and client-facing documents.

## Core Rule

Do not treat copywriting as a one-pass task.

For landing pages, product pages, outreach, proposals, and public-facing material, use this sequence:

1. Clarify the offer.
2. Confirm the audience.
3. Confirm the desired action.
4. Draft the copy.
5. Run the positioning check.
6. Run the wording check.
7. Run the structure check.
8. Produce the final clean version.

```text
Wayne Francis, trading under SF&W Project Solutions
```

```text
Wayne Francis / SF&W Project Solutions
```

## Offer Positioning Rules

### Solo Operator OS

Lead with:
- permanent ownership
- one operating system
- no subscription trap
- less tool switching
- 5-8 hours per week potentially recovered
- personal business operating layer

Avoid leading with:
- AI novelty
- productivity hype
- vague "transformation"
- replacing people
- technical setup

Core message:

```text
Stop renting your business operations. Build the permanent operating system once and own it.
```

### SME AI Automation Audit

Lead with:
- diagnosis before build
- operational pain
- workflow clarity
- risk reduction
- practical roadmap

Avoid implying that the audit includes the full build.

Use this distinction:

```text
The audit identifies what should be improved. The AI OS or implementation build is the separate work that creates the operating system.
```

### Client OS Build And Handover

Lead with:
- custom system
- ownership
- handover
- no vendor lock-in
- faster route than DIY
- cheaper than hiring a developer or operations lead

### Relationship Connector Mode

Lead with:
- post-build optimisation
- keeping operations ahead of growth
- relationship follow-up
- quarterly review
- alternative to hiring operations support too early

Do not position it as basic support.

## Landing Page Structure

Use this structure unless Wayne asks otherwise:

1. Hero
   - clear offer name
   - plain-English outcome
   - one primary CTA
2. Problem
   - operational pain
   - why it matters now
3. What The Product Does
   - concrete capabilities
   - no jargon
4. Who It Is For
   - specific buyer types
5. What You Get
   - deliverables
6. Why It Is Different
   - ownership
   - structure
   - no lock-in
7. How It Works
   - steps
8. Proof / Value Case
   - time saved
   - cost avoided
   - risk reduced
9. Boundaries
   - what is not included
   - professional advice limits
10. CTA
   - simple next action

## Copy Quality Rules

Every final draft must pass these checks:

- Is the offer clear in the first five seconds?
- Is the audience obvious?
- Is the problem specific?
- Is the outcome practical?
- Is the CTA visible and simple?
- Does it avoid hype?
- Does it avoid overpromising?
- Does it distinguish audit from build?
- Does it use Wayne Francis as the brand?
- Does it use SF&W Project Solutions as the trading entity?
- Does it avoid calling Axis AI the trading company?
- Does it avoid unapproved pricing unless marked `in review`?

## Editing Rules

When Wayne asks for edits:

1. Preserve what is working.
2. Identify the specific issue.
3. Make the minimum useful change.
4. Return a clean revised version.
5. Include a short change summary.

Do not rewrite everything unless the structure is broken.

## Language Rules

Use:
- clear
- direct
- practical
- plain English
- calmly confident
- outcome-led

Avoid:
- revolutionary
- game-changing
- unlock your potential
- supercharge
- cutting-edge
- seamless transformation
- next-gen
- AI-powered magic
- vague "efficiency" with no example
