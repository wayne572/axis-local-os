# Usage Tracking Notice

Status: in review
Purpose: Explains how usage may be tracked to improve the client OS.

## Plain-English Summary

This AI OS may track limited usage information so SF&W can support the client, improve workflows, fix issues, and understand which parts of the system are useful.

The goal is improvement, not surveillance.

## What May Be Tracked

Usage tracking should be limited to:

- which modules are used
- dates and times of use
- number of prompts or sessions
- workflow names used
- errors or failed outputs
- support issues
- feature requests
- client-approved feedback
- status changes such as active, in review, locked, archived

## What Should Not Be Tracked For Product Improvement

Do not collect for general product improvement:

- full client messages unless explicitly agreed
- sensitive personal data
- special category data
- passwords or credentials
- private client documents
- personal employee performance monitoring
- client customer data beyond what is needed for the agreed workflow

## Legal Basis

The exact legal basis should be confirmed for each client.

Common routes may include:

- contract: where tracking is needed to provide support and maintain the service
- legitimate interests: where tracking is limited, expected, and used to improve the service
- consent: where optional analytics, testimonials, case studies, or wider product learning are involved

If unsure, mark:

`Compliance Hold`

## Transparency Rule

The client must be told:

- what is tracked
- why it is tracked
- who can see it
- how long it is kept
- how they can opt out of optional tracking
- whether data is anonymised or aggregated

## Improvement Data Rule

SF&W may use anonymised or aggregated learning to improve:

- templates
- workflows
- onboarding
- support
- product design
- training material

SF&W must not use identifiable client data in public material, sales material, case studies, or examples without explicit approval.

## Client Control

The client can ask:

- what usage data is held
- for optional tracking to stop
- for errors to be corrected
- for data to be deleted where legally required

## Retention

Suggested default:

- operational usage logs: 12 months
- support tickets / issue logs: 24 months
- anonymised improvement insights: no fixed deletion date if no person or client can be identified

Confirm per client contract.

## Review Status

This notice must be reviewed before client handover.
