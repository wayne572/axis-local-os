# Wayne Personal Introduction

Status: active template
Purpose: Warm personal introduction from Wayne before a charity/CIC starts using the AI OS.

```text
Hello [Person Name],

My name is Wayne Francis.

I currently run a CIC myself, so I understand how hard it can be to keep everything moving in this sector. The work is meaningful, but it can also be heavy. There is always more to do, more people to support, more funding to think about, more admin to keep on top of, and often not enough time or capacity behind the scenes.

One of my personal goals is to always find ways to give back where I can. I have seen the hard work that goes into running charities, CICs, and community organisations, and I wanted to offer this as a small token of respect for the work you are doing.

This AI OS is not here to replace the heart, judgement, or lived experience inside your organisation. It is here to support you with the background work that often takes up too much time: grants, fundraising, social media, follow-ups, planning, reporting, and keeping important actions visible.

My hope is that it gives you and your team a little more breathing room, helps you stay organised, and supports you to continue the work you are already doing in the community.

There is no pressure to understand everything straight away. Start with rough notes, tell the system what you need help with, and we will keep it simple.

Thank you for the work you do.

Wayne Francis
```

## Tone Rules

Keep the introduction:
- personal
- respectful
- grounded in Wayne's CIC experience
- non-salesy
- clear that this is support, not a replacement for the organisation's judgement
- appreciative of the recipient's work

Avoid:
- overpromising results
- implying the OS will solve everything
- making the charity feel like a test subject
- making the offer sound transactional
- heavy technical language
