# Charity And CIC Data And Safeguarding Boundaries

Status: draft
Purpose: Keep charity/CIC AI OS builds useful without creating unsafe advice, decision, or data-handling risk.

## Core Rule

The AI OS supports administration, coordination, drafting, summarising, and tracking.

It does not replace professional judgement, trustee oversight, safeguarding leads, legal advice, medical advice, financial advice, or service eligibility decisions.

## Allowed Uses

Allowed:
- summarising non-sensitive meeting notes
- drafting routine emails
- creating task lists
- tracking follow-ups
- preparing board updates
- drafting grant content for human review
- anonymising impact stories
- organising volunteer or partner notes
- creating standard operating procedures

## Restricted Uses

Requires human review:
- anything involving a vulnerable person
- safeguarding references
- complaints
- referrals
- eligibility or access to support
- personal stories used publicly
- grant claims or impact statistics
- trustee or board papers
- policy wording
- employment, volunteer, legal, or financial matters

## Never Enter Without A Specific Agreed Process

Do not enter:
- unnecessary personal identifiers
- full addresses for beneficiaries
- medical details
- safeguarding disclosures
- criminal records information
- immigration status
- financial hardship details
- children's details
- passwords, API keys, or private access links

If a workflow needs this type of information, design a minimised version first.

## Anonymisation Standard

Before using sensitive notes in the AI OS:
- remove names
- remove exact addresses
- remove phone numbers and emails
- remove dates of birth
- remove unnecessary case details
- use role labels such as `beneficiary`, `parent`, `volunteer`, or `partner`

## Output Review

All sensitive outputs should be reviewed by:
- the named OS owner
- the safeguarding lead, where relevant
- the charity/CIC lead, where relevant

## Client Handover Warning

The handover must clearly explain:
- what the OS can help with
- what the OS must not decide
- what data should not be entered
- who reviews sensitive outputs
- how to pause use if a concern appears
