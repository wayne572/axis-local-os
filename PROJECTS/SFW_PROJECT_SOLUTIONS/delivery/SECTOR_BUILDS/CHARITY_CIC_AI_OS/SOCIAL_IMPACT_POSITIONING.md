# Social Impact Positioning

Status: active
Purpose: Explain how Axis should present the charity/CIC give-back work publicly without making it feel like a side project or a marketing stunt.

## Core Position

Axis AI is a commercial business with a social-impact commitment from day one.

The business sells AI operating systems to solo operators and SMEs, and reserves capacity each year to support two charities or CICs with donated AI OS builds.

## Public Commitment

Use this wording:

```text
Each year, Axis will support two charities or CICs with a donated AI OS build.

These builds are focused on practical sustainability: grants, fundraising, social media, impact evidence, reporting, volunteer coordination, and weekly operating rhythm.
```

## Value Framing

Do not call the donated builds "free work" without context.

Use:

```text
Donated charity/CIC AI OS build
Estimated implementation value: GBP 3,500-GBP 5,000
```

This protects the value of the work and helps recipients understand they are receiving something substantial.

## Website Placement

The social-impact commitment should appear:
- in the homepage navigation as `Social impact`
- after the proof/system section and before the Wayne/about section
- later, as a dedicated page once there is a first case study or testimonial

## Tone

The tone should be:
- grounded
- personal
- non-performative
- clear about Wayne's CIC experience
- careful not to overpromise outcomes
- focused on helping organisations continue their work

## Avoid

Avoid:
- making the donated builds sound like a trial
- implying charities are a marketing funnel
- overclaiming results before evidence exists
- publishing client stories without permission
- presenting AI as a replacement for care, judgement, or safeguarding

## First Proof To Capture

For each donated build, capture:
- what pressure existed before
- what first win the OS created
- what workflows were built
- what changed after 30 days
- what the organisation would say publicly, if they consent
- what should remain private
