# Charity And CIC AI OS Sector Build

Status: active sector build
Owner: Wayne Francis
Created: 2026-05-14
Purpose: Reusable AI OS build path for charities, community interest companies, and social impact organisations, with a strong focus on grants, fundraising, social media, impact evidence, and long-term sustainability.

## Mission

Help two charities or CICs per year by providing a fully functional Claude AI OS that reduces admin pressure, strengthens follow-up, protects governance, supports grant and fundraising activity, improves social media consistency, and gives small teams a calmer way to continue the work they are doing.

This workstream exists to turn that mission into a repeatable build, not a one-off favour.

## Wayne's Personal Position

Wayne currently runs a CIC himself and understands the pressure of working in this sector.

The offer should be presented as a practical token of respect and support for the hard work charities, CICs, and community organisations already do.

The tone should be:
- personal
- warm
- non-salesy
- respectful
- clear that the OS supports the organisation rather than replacing its people, judgement, or mission

## Who This Is For

Best fit:
- small charities
- community interest companies
- grassroots community organisations
- founder-led or volunteer-led social impact teams
- service delivery teams with limited admin capacity

Poor fit:
- organisations with no named owner for adoption
- teams wanting AI to make safeguarding, legal, medical, financial, or eligibility decisions without human review
- organisations unwilling to define data boundaries
- teams expecting full automation before the manual workflow is proven

## Build Promise

The sector OS should help the organisation:
- capture enquiries, referrals, beneficiary requests, funder opportunities, and internal actions
- maintain a daily or weekly command centre
- draft routine communications in the organisation's voice
- track grants, donors, fundraising campaigns, partners, volunteers, projects, and service delivery work
- prepare grant applications, donor updates, funder reports, fundraising campaigns, and impact evidence for human review
- create social media prompts, image prompts, captions, comments, campaign posts, and content calendars
- protect future funding by tracking deadlines, reporting obligations, renewal dates, and relationship follow-ups
- prepare governance updates and board-ready summaries
- protect sensitive data through clear human review boundaries
- hand the system to non-technical staff with simple operating instructions

## Hidden Support Layer

The OS should contain stronger support tools than it visibly exposes.

These tools help Claude/Axis offer better advice without overwhelming the charity:
- first win selector
- impact evidence vault
- make-this-safer review
- monthly funding rhythm
- board/trustee pack builder
- volunteer activation kit
- local partnership builder
- overwhelmed week mode
- before/after success log
- advice offer filter

These should be used quietly in the background. The charity should experience simple support, not a list of modules.

## Funding Sustainability Engine

Every charity/CIC build should include a funding sustainability layer unless the organisation explicitly does not want it.

The layer covers:
- grant readiness and funder fit checks
- grant opportunity pipeline
- application drafting support
- project budget and evidence checklist
- donor and sponsor relationship follow-up
- fundraising campaign planning
- impact story and outcome evidence capture
- funder reporting deadlines
- renewal and continuation planning
- income risk review

The OS may help organise, draft, summarise, and track funding work.

It must not invent outcomes, exaggerate impact, fabricate evidence, submit applications without human review, or provide regulated financial/legal advice.

## Social Media And Content Engine

Every charity/CIC build should include a social media content layer unless the organisation explicitly does not use social media.

The layer covers:
- weekly content planning
- image-generation prompts
- Canva/design briefs
- captions
- comment replies
- fundraising campaign posts
- donor and sponsor thank-you posts
- volunteer recruitment posts
- impact story posts
- event promotion
- short video/reel scripts
- accessibility alt text
- human-review flags for sensitive posts and comments

The OS may help draft social content, image prompts, captions, comments, and campaign plans.

It must not identify beneficiaries without permission, exploit sensitive stories, fabricate impact, or publish public statements without human review.

The first delivered version should solve the organisation's highest-pressure admin loop before trying to become a full organisation-wide platform.
