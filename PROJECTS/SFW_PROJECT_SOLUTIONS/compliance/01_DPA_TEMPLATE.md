## 1. Definitions

Terms used in this DPA carry the meanings given in the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, including: "Personal Data", "Processing", "Controller", "Processor", "Data Subject", and "Supervisory Authority".

## 2. Subject Matter & Duration

The Processor processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller solely to deliver Pipeline-as-a-Service: lead sourcing, enrichment, outreach, qualification, and booking activities described in the Service Agreement. This DPA runs concurrently with the Service Agreement.

## 3. Nature & Purpose of Processing

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Purpose | B2B lead generation and qualification for the Controller |
| Categories of Data Subjects | Business decision-makers (owners, directors, managers) at UK businesses matching the agreed ICP |
| Categories of Personal Data | Business name, business contact details (email, phone, business address), job title, LinkedIn URL, employer, publicly available business information |
| Special Category Data | None. Special category data must NOT be processed under this DPA |
| Sources | Apollo.io, Common Room, public business directories, the Controller's own data |

## 4. Lawful Basis

The Controller confirms that:
- (a) Its lawful basis for contacting Data Subjects identified by the Processor is **legitimate interest** under Art. 6(1)(f) UK GDPR;
- (b) It has carried out a Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) where required;
- (c) All outreach is **B2B only** and role-relevant;
- (d) It will not use the Personal Data for B2C marketing, automated decision-making with legal effect, or any high-risk AI use case.

## 5. Processor Obligations

The Processor shall:
1. Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller.
2. Ensure all personnel processing the data are bound by confidentiality.
3. Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures (Schedule 1).
4. Not engage sub-processors without prior written authorisation (Schedule 2).
5. Assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject Requests within 5 working days.
6. Notify the Controller of any Personal Data Breach within 24 hours of becoming aware.
7. Maintain a written record of all processing activities under Art. 30(2) UK GDPR.
8. On termination, delete or return all Personal Data within 30 days, save where retention is required by law.

## 6. Suppression & Opt-Out

The Processor maintains a master Suppression List. Any Data Subject who:
- requests opt-out via the unsubscribe mechanism in any outreach,
- submits a Subject Access Request requesting erasure,
- is identified as having objected to direct marketing,

is added to the Suppression List within 1 working day and is not contacted again across any client engagement.

## 7. International Transfers

No Personal Data is transferred outside the UK or EEA except via providers covered by an adequacy decision or appropriate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses + UK Addendum). Current sub-processors and their hosting locations are listed in Schedule 2.

## 8. Audit Rights

The Controller may, on 30 days' written notice and no more than once per 12-month period, audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA. Costs of routine audits sit with the Controller; costs of audits triggered by a confirmed breach sit with the Processor.

## 9. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA is governed by the liability provisions of the underlying Service Agreement.

## 10. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the laws of England and Wales. Disputes are subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts.

## Schedule 1 - Technical & Organisational Measures

- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) for all data exchange.
- Encryption at rest for stored Personal Data.
- Access control: role-based, MFA enforced on all admin accounts.
- Backup: daily, retained for 30 days.
- Suppression List: encrypted, single source of truth, reviewed weekly.
- Personnel training: GDPR + PECR refresher annually.
- Incident response plan with 24-hour breach notification SLA.
