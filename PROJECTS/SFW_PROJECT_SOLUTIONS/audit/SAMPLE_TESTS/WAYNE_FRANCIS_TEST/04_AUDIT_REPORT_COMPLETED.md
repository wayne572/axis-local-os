## 1. Executive Summary

This audit reviewed the lead-to-client workflow for a small consultancy where enquiries, follow-up, proposals, and onboarding are currently handled across several tools and manual notes.

Main finding:
The business does not need a large system first. It needs one clean operating flow from enquiry to proposal to onboarding.

Recommended first improvement:
Build a lightweight Lead-to-Proposal Command Centre.

Expected benefit:
Fewer missed follow-ups, faster proposals, clearer next actions, and a cleaner handover when a client says yes.

## 3. Key Findings

### Finding 1

Issue:
Enquiries arrive across too many channels without one central record.

Why it matters:
When leads are scattered, follow-up depends on memory. This increases the risk of slow replies and lost opportunities.

Recommended action:
Create a simple lead tracker with source, pain point, next action, follow-up date, and status.

### Finding 2

Issue:
Discovery notes are not consistently captured in a format that supports proposal writing.

Why it matters:
Proposal drafting takes longer when call notes are incomplete or scattered.

Recommended action:
Use a standard discovery notes template that captures pain, workflow, buying signals, and next step.

### Finding 3

Issue:
Client onboarding and delivery handover are too manual.

Why it matters:
Once a client says yes, the business needs a repeatable way to move from proposal to delivery without losing scope or context.

Recommended action:
Use a handover checklist and client setup workflow based on the Client OS Template.

## 6. Recommended First Build

Recommended project:
Lead-to-Proposal Command Centre

Problem solved:
Scattered enquiries, inconsistent follow-up, slow proposal drafting, and manual onboarding.

Minimum viable version:
One structured folder or tracker that manages:
- leads
- discovery notes
- follow-ups
- proposal drafting
- client onboarding next steps

What it would do:
- capture leads from different sources
- record next action and follow-up date
- turn discovery notes into proposal inputs
- prepare handover steps after acceptance

Tools involved:
Claude, email, LinkedIn, documents, WhatsApp capture later.

Client input needed:
Recent enquiry examples, current proposal format, onboarding steps, and preferred follow-up rhythm.

Estimated build complexity:
low to medium
