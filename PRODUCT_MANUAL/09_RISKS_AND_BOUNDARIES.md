# 09 - Risks And Boundaries

Status: active draft
Purpose: Make product limits clear.

## Main Risks

### 1. Legal And Compliance Risk

AXIS may help draft, organise, and prepare documents.

It must not be positioned as legal advice.

Client-facing compliance wording should be reviewed before use.

### 2. Personal Data Risk

AXIS may store client context, relationship notes, admin notes, and usage logs.

Only collect what is needed.

Be clear about:
- what is tracked
- why it is tracked
- who can see it
- how long it is kept
- how the client can request deletion

### 3. Professional Advice Risk

Solo Operator OS may touch personal life, admin, money, health, and decisions.

It must not replace:
- medical advice
- legal advice
- regulated financial advice
- tax advice
- therapy
- safeguarding support

### 4. Over-Automation Risk

Clients may expect the OS to run everything without review.

The correct message:
AXIS handles structure, drafts, prompts, workflows, and follow-through support. Humans still approve high-risk decisions.

### 5. Scope Creep

The system can do many things.

The commercial path should stay focused:
1. audit
2. workflow selection
3. client OS build
4. handover
5. 30-day review

### 6. Channel Risk

Telegram and WhatsApp should not become uncontrolled data stores.

They should pass useful capture into AXIS with clear boundaries.

### 7. Product Clarity Risk

Do not describe AXIS as magic.

Describe it as:
- structured AI operating system
- workflow layer
- client handover system
- personal / business command centre

## Required Client Boundary

Use this wording as the base:

```text
AXIS supports organisation, planning, drafting, workflow management, and decision clarity. It does not replace professional legal, medical, financial, tax, therapeutic, safeguarding, or regulated advice.
```

## Internal Rule

If a claim sounds too strong, soften it.

If a workflow touches sensitive data, add a boundary.

If an output affects money, health, law, employment, or safeguarding, require human review.
