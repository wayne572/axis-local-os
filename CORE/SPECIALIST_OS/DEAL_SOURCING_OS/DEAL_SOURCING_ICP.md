# DEAL_SOURCING_ICP.md

## 1. Status
Active - Execution Sub-File (Canonical ICP - merged)

---

## 2. Purpose
Define how the user identifies the right people to target.

This prevents:

- random outreach
- low-quality leads
- wasted time

---

## 3. Core Principle

Clear targeting = better results.

Sourcing starts with clarity, not volume. A small accurate list always beats a large vague one.

---

## 4. Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

The user must define:

- Industry / sector
- Business size (revenue, headcount)
- Location (important for GDPR / PECR)
- Decision-maker role
- Core problem they solve
- Trigger events (what makes them ready to buy - hiring, funding, leadership change, regulation)
- Ability to pay (qualitative - high / mid / low)
- Exclusion criteria (who NOT to target)

---

## 5. ICP Builder (Simple Format)

Claude should guide the user through:

- Who do you help?
- What problem do they have?
- What size is their business?
- Where are they based?
- Who makes the decision?
- What event makes them ready to buy now?
- Who are you NOT going after?

---

## 6. Build Steps

1. Define the problem you solve in one sentence.
2. Identify who has that problem most acutely.
3. Narrow to a sector + role + size combination.
4. Add geography and language.
5. Define trigger events.
6. List exclusions explicitly.

---

## 7. Example ICP

- Industry: Local service businesses
- Size: 1-10 employees
- Location: UK
- Role: Owner / Founder
- Problem: inconsistent lead flow
- Trigger: recently hired first salesperson, or just lost a key client
- Budget: able to spend GBP 500-GBP 2,000/month
- Exclusion: pure B2C consumer brands, AML-regulated sectors without CDD in place

---

## 8. Compliance Rules (UK GDPR / PECR)

The ICP must:

- be relevant to your service
- be justifiable under "legitimate interest" (B2B default)
- not include sensitive personal data
- focus on business contact context

Hard stops - exclude these by default unless explicitly cleared:

- B2C without explicit consent (PECR)
- AML-regulated sectors (accounting, legal, estate agency, tax, crypto) without CDD
- High-risk verticals (recruitment screening, credit, medical, biometric) - requires Wayne sign-off

---

## 9. Validation

Before moving to lead identification, confirm:

- ICP fits within UK compliance defaults
- exclusion list is honest (not aspirational)
- profile is narrow enough to act on
- one-sentence test passes: "Does this person match the profile?"

---

## 10. Output Requirement

The ICP must be:

- written down as a one-page profile
- clearly defined
- simple
- specific enough to act on
- a living document (updated as data comes in)

---

## 11. Failure Conditions

The ICP has failed if:

- it is too broad
- it includes "any business"
- it lacks a clear problem
- it cannot guide outreach
- exclusions are missing
- compliance defaults are ignored

---

## 12. End State

The ICP is working when:

- the user knows exactly who to target
- outreach feels focused
- leads are relevant and higher quality
- every prospect can be tested with one question: "Does this match?"

If yes -> progress.
If no -> drop without guilt.
